Project Plan

Overview

Utilising voice assistance (VA) and machine learning technology to interface with an organisations knowledge management system in call centres. Using this technology will create efficiencies within the organisation, and a better overall customer experience.

Motivation

Call centres operators provide advice to customers which can be of a complex nature, often requiring support from a supervisor or a subject matter expert, or manually searching a knowledge management system. While sourcing this information, customers are placed on hold, increasing the total call time and decreasing customer satisfaction.

Stats show average hold time for an established agent is 10 minutes, for new starters, this can blow out to 60 minutes. With 10’s of thousands of calls per day, saving anytime per call will have a huge impact with the volume of calls.

Description

Voice Assistant to listen to calls while the customer is speaking, in real time, the VA searches the knowledge management system and brings up the relevant articles for the issue. This will allow the staff member to focus on the issue at hand, give an immediate response to the query.

Machine learning will also capture this information and store. This will then be used to inform staff coaching, including, information given, tone with customer, etc.

As the volume of calls come though the ML will build its own knowledge base, and provide immediate responses. Overtime, this could phase out the requirement for an agent to take less complex calls. The agent could then focus on a case management approach, or be redeployed to other areas.

Tools and Technologies

  • Servers
  • Databases
  • VA software
  • Option to product stack for easier configuration

Skills Required

  • Hardware technician to set up servers
  • Database managers
  • Software developer to integrate the voice assistance technology with knowledge management system
  • Cyber security expert to ensure data protection

Outcome

Success of this project will result in a better customer experience, greater support for staff, less overheads required. The machine learning aspect will learn to complete less complex queries end to end, freeing up staff to be redeployed to other critical areas in the organisation.
